# OFRENDA — los 8 prompts, versión set

**Deep house · 121 BPM constante · estilo Boiler Room Tulum**

Cambio contra `ALBUM-OFRENDA.md`: aquel disco era tech house a 128 en tonalidades
mayores. Este es un **set de Solomun**, así que baja a 121, se vuelve deep house y
casi todo pasa a menor. **Se invierte la simetría**: en el brief ECLIPSE era la
única en menor; aquí **AURORA es la única en mayor.** Un solo momento de luz en un
disco oscuro es más fuerte que un solo momento de sombra en uno luminoso — y le
sirve mejor a la narrativa, porque la ofrenda es gris y la respuesta es la luz.

## El arco armónico (Camelot)

Todas a 121 BPM para que se puedan mezclar sin estirar nada.

| # | rola | tono | Camelot | por qué ahí |
|---|------|------|---------|-------------|
| 01 | PETRICOR | Fa menor | 4A | abre |
| 02 | MONZÓN | Do menor | 5A | +1 |
| 03 | BIOLUMINISCENCIA | Sol menor | 6A | +1 |
| 04 | MURMURACIÓN | Re menor | 7A | +1 |
| 05 | AURORA | **Fa mayor** | **7B** | mismo número, cambia la letra — **se prende la luz sin cambiar de tono** |
| 06 | ECLIPSE | Re menor | 7A | vuelve exactamente de donde vino: se apaga |
| 07 | SALAR | La menor | 8A | +1, sin voz |
| 08 | CENIZA | Fa menor | 4A | cierra el círculo en el tono de la 01 |

El truco está en el 05→06. Pasar de menor a su relativa mayor es el mismo número
de Camelot con otra letra: se mezcla perfecto y suena a que alguien encendió algo.
Y ECLIPSE regresa al mismo número, o sea que la luz se va por donde llegó.

## Reglas que van en todos

- `121 BPM` — no negociable, es lo que hace mezclable el set
- `keep the top end restrained and slightly dark` — la primera prueba salió a 3565 Hz de centroide, arriba del rango comercial
- `warm, analog bass, never distorted or aggressive` — el bajo saturado ya se rechazó dos veces
- `no big drop, no buildup cliches` — Solomun construye por acumulación, no por caída
- nunca nombrar artistas ni canciones existentes

## 02 · MONZÓN — Do menor
La lluvia que llega toda de golpe después de meses.

```
Create an instrumental deep house track. 121 BPM, C minor. Title: MONZON.
MOOD: months of waiting, then all the rain at once. Release after tension. Physical and rolling.
GROOVE: soft rounded house kick, four on the floor. Percussion-forward — congas weaving between the kicks, shakers, woodblocks, all rolling and human. Recorded in a real room with air around the drums, not programmed. Warm analog bassline with a slow audible filter movement, rounded and never distorted.
HARMONY: warm organ stabs answering the percussion. Minor 7th chords, short and dry. A sustained pedal tone holds above every chord change.
VOICE: one wordless sustained female vowel, a single held note that rises with the energy. No lyrics, no words. It is atmosphere, not a hook.
ARRANGEMENT: 6 minutes. Steadily thickening — the rain gets heavier by adding percussion, never by a drop. No buildup cliches.
MIX: warm and mid focused. Keep the top end restrained and slightly dark. Groove over brightness.
```

## 03 · BIOLUMINISCENCIA — Sol menor
El mar que se enciende cuando lo tocas.

```
Create an instrumental deep house track. 121 BPM, G minor. Title: BIOLUMINISCENCIA.
MOOD: black water that lights up green where you touch it. Liquid, glowing, responsive.
GROOVE: rounded house kick, tight and soft. Rolling warm analog bassline in the pocket with the kick. Swung hi-hats and open hats with a loose human feel.
HARMONY: bright plucky stabs with delay, short and wet, sparkling in the gaps.
VOICE: a chopped wordless female vocal used as the main rhythmic hook — stuttered, pitched, multiplied by delay so it echoes into the spaces. No lyrics. The vocal IS the melody.
ARRANGEMENT: 5 to 6 minutes. Everything feels like it lights up in response to the groove. Confident and physical but never harsh.
MIX: warm, wet, spacious. Keep the top end restrained and slightly dark despite the sparkle.
```

## 04 · MURMURACIÓN — Re menor
Cien mil estorninos volando como un solo cuerpo.

```
Create an instrumental deep house track. 121 BPM, D minor. Title: MURMURACION.
MOOD: a hundred thousand birds moving as one body. No single leader — many small parts, one motion.
GROOVE: layered percussion where no single element leads. Dozens of tiny rhythmic details weaving together. Soft rounded kick, rolling warm bass, dry hand percussion. Restrained and hypnotic.
HARMONY: sparse warm chords with a lot of space around them. A pedal tone underneath everything.
VOICE: a wordless vocal multiplied into many slightly offset copies of itself, breathing together like a flock. No lyrics.
ARRANGEMENT: 6 minutes. This is the most detailed track on the record — reward close listening. Nothing loud, everything moving.
MIX: spacious, warm, mid focused. Keep the top end restrained and slightly dark.
```

## 05 · AURORA — Fa mayor · **la única en mayor**
El cielo prendido en verde. El pico del set.

```
Create an instrumental deep house track. 121 BPM, F major. Title: AURORA.
MOOD: the sky catching fire in green. The peak of the record — bright, vast, transcendent without being cheesy. This is the only major-key track here and it should feel like light arriving.
GROOVE: driving rolling groove underneath keeps it a club track, not an ambient piece. Soft rounded kick, warm analog bass, swung hats.
HARMONY: big warm chords opening up. A detuned lead with long delay riding over the bass. Major but never saccharine — restrained, patient, enormous.
VOICE: a wordless choir of open vowels in huge reverb, arriving like light. No lyrics, no words.
ARRANGEMENT: 6 to 7 minutes. Big but controlled. It swells rather than drops. No buildup cliches.
MIX: wide and warm with real air. Keep the top end restrained and slightly dark even at the peak.
```

## 06 · ECLIPSE — Re menor
Oscuridad a mediodía. Los animales se callan.

```
Create an instrumental deep house track. 121 BPM, D minor. Title: ECLIPSE.
MOOD: darkness at midday. The groove keeps going but the light goes out. Tense, muted, unsettling.
GROOVE: deep rolling warm bass, minimal percussion, soft kick. Everything filtered and pulled back, as if heard through a wall.
HARMONY: almost none — one dark sustained tone and a filtered chord that never resolves.
VOICE: a low whispered wordless voice sitting very low in the mix, barely audible. No lyrics.
ARRANGEMENT: 6 minutes. Do not resolve. End it still dark.
MIX: dark, narrow, close. Keep the top end restrained. Warm bass, never distorted.
```

## 07 · SALAR — La menor · **la única sin voz**
El salar después de la lluvia: el cielo reflejado completo.

```
Create an instrumental deep house track. 121 BPM, A minor. Title: SALAR.
MOOD: a salt flat under a film of water, sky reflected perfectly — earth and sky become the same thing. Wide, still, reflective.
GROOVE: rolling but unhurried. Soft kick, warm analog bass, drum-line style percussion rolling underneath.
HARMONY: a cosmic detuned lead with long delay carries the entire melody. Confident and architectural.
VOICE: no vocals at all. None. The melody carries everything — this track proves the record works without a voice.
ARRANGEMENT: 6 minutes. Spacious, patient, a lot of air.
MIX: very wide, warm, lots of space. Keep the top end restrained and slightly dark.
```

## 08 · CENIZA — Fa menor
Lo que queda de la ofrenda. Cierra en el tono en que abrió.

```
Create an instrumental deep house track. 121 BPM, F minor. Title: CENIZA.
MOOD: the comedown. Volcanic ash circling the world and landing on another continent. Nostalgic without being sad.
GROOVE: starts with the full rolling groove and slowly removes elements until only percussion and a pad remain.
HARMONY: one warm pad holding underneath, fading across the track.
VOICE: a single processed human exhale, heavily reverbed, appearing and fading — the answer to the breath that opened the record. No words.
ARRANGEMENT: 7 minutes. The last two minutes should feel like walking away. End on percussion only, then silence.
MIX: warm, soft, distant. Keep the top end restrained and slightly dark.
```
