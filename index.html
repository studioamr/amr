THE SOUND

Sub grave, dust, and patience.

This sound was born on a PLINTH — the sculptural DJ booths cast as one object. Music built for monuments has rules: the sub sits at 41–65 Hz where you feel it in your chest, the needle dust stays in the mix because the surface is part of the song, and every chord waits two bars before it answers.

No presets. No samples. Every kick, pad and grain of dust on these records was synthesized from raw sine waves and noise — then cut to the groove.

WHO IS AMR

AMR are the initials of André Macouzet Ruiz — a builder from Morelia who treats every release like architecture.

The same hands behind the PLINTH monoliths cut these five records. The rule is the one pinned to the studio wall: most DJ booths are furniture — this is a monument. Most tracks are content — these are records. Each one is numbered by hand. None will be repressed.
