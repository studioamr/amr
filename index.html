    OUT NOW — NEW RELEASE

    COLIBRÍ

    A left turn — French-touch tropical in the spirit of Polo & Pan. Eight movements at 120 BPM in bright major keys: marimba, pan flute, glassy arpeggios through tape delay, happy vocal chops, a funky bassline — and that bittersweet major-to-minor turn that makes the sun feel nostalgic. Water and birds all the way through. Cut to turquoise vinyl, numbered by hand.

    OUT NOW — NEW RELEASE

    JACARANDA

    The violet season. Ten movements at 120 BPM under the jacaranda in bloom — organic percussion played loose, supersaw pads opening like a canopy, a kalimba raining like petals. Energy moves in seasons, not drops: the shade is on purpose. Cut to violet vinyl, numbered by hand.

    OUT NOW — NEW RELEASE

    GUERRERO

    Written for the desert. Twelve new cuts at 123 BPM where the bassline is the lead — saw, sub and acid squelch through a filter that opens all night. Soft kicks, hand percussion, semitone tension. Zero ceremony, constant groove. Cut to jade vinyl, numbered by hand.

    THE NIGHT SET — MMXXVI

    DELIRIO

    One night that won't let go. A continuous set — 1h51, sixteen movements in a single take, tempo-locked transitions, in sync from the first bar to the last. Cut to blue vinyl, numbered by hand.

    THE DEBUT RELEASE — MMXXVI

    MONUMENTS

    Where it all started. Five cuts synthesized from pure waveforms — no samples, no presets — pressed to amber vinyl. Sub you feel in your chest, dust pressed into the groove, chords that answer late.

    THE VINYL

    Amber, wine, blue, jade, violet, turquoise.

    The collection, six records deep: the amber EP, the session on wine-red, DELIRIO on blue, GUERRERO on jade and JACARANDA on violet. Hit play on a record — it plays right here and spins. When an edition sells out, the plate is retired.

    WHO IS STUDIO AMR

    Studio AMR is the workshop of André Macouzet Ruiz — AMR, his initials — a builder from Morelia who treats every release like architecture.

    Six records so far: an EP pressed from pure waveforms, three continuous sets, and JACARANDA the hypnotic one, out now. The rule is the one pinned to the studio wall: most tracks are content — these are records. Each one is numbered by hand. None will be repressed.
