    OUT NOW — NEW RELEASE

    SUBSUELO

    A Boiler Room is not a show — it is a room, a low ceiling, the DJ at floor level, and the music is the only thing happening. So this record has no voices: nobody is talking down there. Eight levels straight down at 125 BPM, one continuous mix — from the kerb, where you only hear the low end through a closed door, to BASALTO, the bedrock the whole city stands on, then the water seeps back and a door opens onto daylight. The bassline leads the whole way. Concrete, iron and groove. Cut to rust-on-concrete vinyl, numbered by hand.

    OUT NOW — NEW RELEASE

    JACARANDA

    The jacaranda is not interesting as a tree. It is interesting as an event: it turns a whole city violet for three weeks and then it is over. Seven movements at 120 BPM in one continuous mix, read as omen and blaze and vestige rather than as a season — VÍSPERA before anything shows, AUGURIO for the first flower, FULGOR when the whole tree catches, VESTIGIO for what is left on the ground. Deep house, patient, no ceremony. Cut to violet vinyl, numbered by hand.

    OUT NOW — NEW RELEASE

    GUERRERO

    Written for the desert. Twelve new cuts at 123 BPM where the bassline is the lead — saw, sub and acid squelch through a filter that opens all night. Soft kicks, hand percussion, semitone tension. Zero ceremony, constant groove. Cut to jade vinyl, numbered by hand.

    THE NIGHT SET — MMXXVI

    DELIRIO

    One night that won't let go. A continuous set — 1h51, sixteen movements in a single take, tempo-locked transitions, in sync from the first bar to the last. Cut to blue vinyl, numbered by hand.

    THE DEBUT RELEASE — MMXXVI

    MONUMENTS

    Where it all started. Five cuts synthesized from pure waveforms — no samples, no presets — pressed to amber vinyl. Sub you feel in your chest, dust pressed into the groove, chords that answer late.

    WHO IS STUDIO AMR

    Studio AMR is the workshop of André Macouzet Ruiz — AMR, his initials — a builder from Morelia who treats every release like architecture.

    Six records: an EP pressed from pure waveforms, three continuous sets, JACARANDA — a flowering tree read as an event — and SUBSUELO, eight levels of voiceless Boiler Room tech house down to bedrock. Plus cuts from JACARANDA and SUBSUELO. The rule is the one pinned to the studio wall: most tracks are content — these are records. Each one is numbered by hand. None will be repressed.
